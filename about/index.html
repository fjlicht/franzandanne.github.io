About

I have studied a mix of biology: ecological, computational, and pathological which provides me with a unique perspective in my current research.

An alumnus of Pennsylvania State University, I have explored numerous research fields, from mycorrhizal fungi and bacterial pathogens of cucurbits with multiple mentors at Penn State to human pathology at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research.

I now work on agricultural plant pathogens, particularily fungal pathogens of potato and inter species interactions.

I began my graduate studies at the the University of New Hampshire, where I focused on soil microbial community dynamics associated with perennial grass systems in the northeastern US.

Here I recieved an opportunity to research abroad in Senegal, Western Africa for a National Science Foundation fellowship to study soil microbial ecology, partnering with US, French, and Senegalese Researchers.

The Broders lab then moved west to CSU and I followed to continue my research and PhD.

Now I live in Fort Collins, Colorado and love the beautiful weather and gorgeous skies.

With my personal time I get outside, work on my current research interests and evolving recreational activities.
